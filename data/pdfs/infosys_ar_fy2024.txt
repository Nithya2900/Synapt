INFOSYS LIMITED — ANNUAL REPORT FY2024
Management Discussion and Analysis

COMPANY OVERVIEW
Infosys Limited is a global leader in next-generation digital services and consulting. For the fiscal year ended March 31, 2024, Infosys reported revenues of ₹153,670 crore, reflecting a challenging macroeconomic environment with clients cautious on discretionary technology spending.

OPERATING PERFORMANCE
The operating margin for FY2024 stood at 20.7%, a slight decline from 21.0% in FY2023. Management attributed the margin compression primarily to elevated employee costs following large-scale hiring in prior years and wage revision cycles. The company undertook targeted cost optimization programs including facility consolidation and pyramid correction to partially offset these headwinds.

Net profit for FY2024 was ₹26,248 crore, up from ₹24,108 crore in FY2023, supported by other income and improved tax efficiencies.

STRATEGIC PRIORITIES
1. Generative AI and Automation: Infosys launched its Topaz AI platform, embedding generative AI capabilities across service lines. Over 50,000 employees have been trained on generative AI tools. The company secured AI-led deal wins exceeding $1 billion in total contract value during the year.

2. Cloud and Digital Transformation: The company continued to invest in its Cobalt cloud platform, helping clients migrate to hyperscaler environments. Cloud revenues grew 25% year-on-year.

3. Cost Efficiency: Infosys implemented a voluntary separation scheme and restructured its real estate footprint, reducing costs by approximately ₹1,200 crore on an annualized basis.

4. Large Deal Wins: The company reported large deal wins of $17.7 billion in FY2024, one of its highest ever, providing strong revenue visibility for the coming years.

HEADCOUNT AND TALENT
Employee headcount stood at 317,240 as of March 2024, a reduction of approximately 26,000 from the peak of 343,234 in FY2023. Attrition declined to 12.7% from 20.9% in the prior year, reflecting stabilization in the talent market and reduced hiring by peers.

SEGMENT PERFORMANCE
Financial Services contributed 27% of revenues, Manufacturing 15%, Retail 14%, Communication 12%, and Energy-Utilities-Resources-Services 13%. The Financial Services segment faced headwinds due to reduced discretionary spending by banking clients navigating higher interest rate environments.

OUTLOOK
Management guided for revenue growth of 1–3% in constant currency for FY2025. The company remains cautious on near-term demand but confident in secular AI-driven technology spending over a 3–5 year horizon.

DIVIDEND AND CAPITAL ALLOCATION
The Board declared a final dividend of ₹20 per share, taking the total dividend for FY2024 to ₹36 per share. Infosys also completed a share buyback of ₹9,300 crore during the year.

RISKS
Key risks include client concentration in the US and European markets, currency fluctuation, talent retention, and increased competition from hyperscalers entering the IT services space. The company has implemented hedging policies covering approximately 85% of net foreign exchange exposure.
